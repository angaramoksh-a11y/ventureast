The long view

Venture, before it was fashionable.

Ventureast began in 1997, when Sarath Naru's team won the bid to privatise the venture arm of APIDC — the first privatisation of a financial institution in India. A $10 million first fund became six funds, a series of Indian firsts, and one of the longest track records in Indian venture.

  1. 1997

    Born from a first

    The team wins the privatisation of APIDC's venture arm — India's first privatised financial institution — and raises its first ~$10M fund.

  2. 2003

    India's first life-sciences fund

    The Biotechnology Venture Fund, backed by the IFC, a decade before "healthtech" had a name.

  3. 2006

    India's first academic seed fund

    The Tenet Fund, in partnership with IIT Madras' TeNeT group — university research to company, systematically.

  4. 2008

    Proactive Fund I

    A ~$100M technology fund with the IFC as anchor — early-stage conviction at institutional scale.

  5. 2011

    Climate, agri & health — early

    Ventureast Life Fund III launches with a $20M commitment from the Asian Development Bank.

  6. 2016

    The sixth fund

    Proactive Fund II closes near $100M, anchored by global development-finance institutions.

  7. 2023

    Zaggle rings the bell

    A long-held conviction lists on the NSE & BSE.

  8. 2024

    100x, realised

    Full exit from Zaggle — one of the largest returns in Indian venture history.

  9. 2026

    Kissht lists

    Another portfolio IPO on the NSE & BSE. The long view keeps paying.
